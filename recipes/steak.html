Seared Black Pepper Steak with Creamy Mushroom-Spinach Saute

A delicious and nutritious steak recipe with spinach to make you strong.

This steak recipe has great balance with vegetables while adding some flavor with a nice light sauce on the veggies.

Ingredients

  • 16 oz frozen chopped spinach
  • 8 fl oz heavy whipping cream
  • 2 shallots
  • 16 oz sliced white mushrooms
  • 1 1/2 lb New York strip steak
  • salt, black pepper, and garlic powder, to taste
  • butter, unsalted

Steps

  1. Preheat a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. While the skillet heats up, pat the steak dry with paper towels and place on a plate. Season with salt and pepper on both sides.
  3. Once the skillet is hot, add butter and swirl to coat the bottom. Add steak and cook until desired doneness, 2-5 minutes per side. Transfer steak to a clean plate, loosely cover with aluminum foil, and set aside to rest.
  4. Meanwhile, place spinach in a colander and rinse under hot, running water (from the tap) to defrost; set aside to drain further.
  5. Peel and mince shallots.
  6. When the steak is done, wipe the skillet clean with paper towels. Return to medium-high heat.
  7. Once the skillet is hot, add more butter and swirl to coat the bottom. Add shallot and mushrooms; cook, stirring occasionally, until mushrooms soften, 2-3 minutes.
  8. Add spinach, cream, and spices to the skillet; stir to combine and bring to a simmer. Once simmering, reduce heat to low and cook until spinach is warmed through and sauce thickens slightly, 1-2 minutes. Remove from heat.
  9. Thinly slice steak against the grain.
  10. To serve, divide steak and creamy veggie saute between plates. Enjoy!
